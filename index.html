AI and ML Timeline

This timeline highlights the significant milestones in AI and ML fields, demonstrating my understanding of AI/ML history and how these milestones impact modern technology.

  • 1956 - The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined by John McCarthy during the Dartmouth Conference.
  • 1960s - ELIZA, one of the first chatbot programs, was created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT.
  • 1980s - Backpropagation became widely used for training neural networks.
  • 1997 - IBM’s Deep Blue defeated chess champion Garry Kasparov, marking a major milestone in AI and game-playing algorithms.
  • 2006 - The concept of Deep Learning became popular, leading to breakthroughs in AI/ML applications.
  • 2012 - AlexNet revolutionized computer vision by achieving remarkable results in the ImageNet competition.
  • 2019 - OpenAI's GPT-2 model showcased significant advancements in natural language processing.
  • 2020 - GPT-3 was released, showcasing major improvements in language generation and understanding.

Reflection

Creating this artifact deepened my understanding of the enormous infrastructure required to train state-of-the-art AI systems. I was particularly struck by the scale of compute and energy costs — training a single frontier model like GPT-4 can consume as much energy as thousands of homes use in a year. This exercise also helped me appreciate why safety alignment phases like RLHF and Anthropic's Constitutional AI are not optional add-ons but integral parts of the training pipeline. Understanding these cost structures is essential for anyone involved in AI development or investment decisions, and this project has sharpened my ability to communicate complex technical concepts visually to diverse audiences.
