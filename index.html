AI and ML Timeline

This timeline highlights the significant milestones in the **AI** and **ML** fields, demonstrating my understanding of AI/ML history and how these milestones impact modern technology.

  • 1956 - The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined by John McCarthy during the **Dartmouth Conference**.
  • 1960s - **ELIZA**, one of the first chatbot programs, was created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT.
  • 1980s - **Backpropagation** became widely used for training neural networks.
  • 1997 - IBM’s **Deep Blue** defeated chess champion Garry Kasparov, marking a major milestone in AI and game-playing algorithms.
  • 2006 - The concept of **Deep Learning** became popular, leading to breakthroughs in AI/ML applications.
  • 2012 - **AlexNet** revolutionized the field of computer vision by achieving remarkable results in the **ImageNet competition**.
  • 2019 - OpenAI's **GPT-2** model showcased significant advancements in natural language processing.
  • 2020 - **GPT-3** (the model behind tools like ChatGPT) was released, showcasing massive improvements in language generation and understanding.
