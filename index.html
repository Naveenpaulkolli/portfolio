AI and ML Timeline

This timeline highlights the significant milestones in AI and ML fields, demonstrating my understanding of AI/ML history and how these milestones impact modern technology.

  • 1956 - The term "Artificial Intelligence" was coined by John McCarthy during the Dartmouth Conference.
  • 1960s - ELIZA, one of the first chatbot programs, was created by Joseph Weizenbaum at MIT.
  • 1980s - Backpropagation became widely used for training neural networks.
  • 1997 - IBM’s Deep Blue defeated chess champion Garry Kasparov, marking a major milestone in AI and game-playing algorithms.
  • 2006 - The concept of Deep Learning became popular, leading to breakthroughs in AI/ML applications.
  • 2012 - AlexNet revolutionized computer vision by achieving remarkable results in the ImageNet competition.
  • 2019 - OpenAI's GPT-2 model showcased significant advancements in natural language processing.
  • 2020 - GPT-3 was released, showcasing major improvements in language generation and understanding.

Generative AI LLM Infrastructure: Training Process and Costs

This artifact explains how large language models like GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, and LLaMA are trained. It also shows the main resources required such as data, compute power, energy, and time.

Training Process

  • Data Collection: Data is collected from websites, books, and code repositories.
  • Data Processing: Data is cleaned and converted into tokens.
  • Model Training: The model learns patterns using GPUs or TPUs.
  • Fine-Tuning: The model is improved using human feedback.
  • Evaluation: The model is tested for accuracy.
  • Deployment: The model is used in real applications.

Resource Costs

  • Data: Billions of words collected from multiple sources
  • Compute: Thousands of GPUs (very expensive)
  • Energy: High electricity usage in data centers
  • Time: Training takes weeks or months

Examples of Models

  • GPT-4 (OpenAI)
  • Claude (Anthropic)
  • Gemini (Google)
  • LLaMA (Meta)

Model Explainability & Decision Matrix Artifact

This artifact compares different pre-trained AI models across domains such as NLP, Computer Vision, and Tabular Data. The goal is to analyze trade-offs between model size, accuracy, speed, and explainability to help select the right model for real-world applications.

Decision Matrix

Model Domain Model Size Accuracy Speed Explainability Best Use Case
GPT-4 NLP / Generative AI Very Large Very High Medium Low Chatbots, content generation
BERT NLP Large High Medium Medium Sentiment analysis, Q&A
MobileNet Computer Vision Small Good Very Fast Medium Mobile image classification
EfficientNet Computer Vision Medium Very High Fast Low High-accuracy image tasks
XGBoost Tabular Data Small Very High Fast High Fraud detection, prediction

Key Insights

  • Large models like GPT-4 provide high accuracy but lower explainability.
  • Lightweight models like MobileNet are faster and suitable for real-time applications.
  • Tabular models like XGBoost offer better interpretability and are easier to explain.
  • Model selection depends on balancing performance, speed, and explainability.
